Subgraphs — rebrand explorations

Three directions for the Subgraphs sub-brand. Parent Second Layer identity stays editorial/minimal; Subgraphs gets its own playful energy, jam.dev-adjacent, committed fully to one accent per direction.

Product
Subgraphs. Turn onchain events into queryable Postgres tables. defineSubgraph({ sources, schema, handlers }).
Reference
jam.dev — restrained playfulness, one ownable accent, product-as-hero, community-noun move, laminated shimmer on the emotional word.
Scope
Sub-brand only. Parent secondlayer.tools identity unchanged. This is a landing-page rebrand, not a system rewrite.
Constraint
Each direction commits to ONE accent color. No purple gradients, no Inter-only pairings, no centered-sans-on-white. Real product copy, real screenshots, real code.

How to pick

Pick A if…

You want jam.dev's feeling but an accent nobody else in blockchain has taken. Safest-bold direction — familiar dark-dev-tool language, distinctive in palette only. Ships fast.

Risk: may read as "a dark dev tool with orange" — leans on craft to feel different.

Pick B if…

You want the brand people quote. Dex, chapters, hand-drawn circles, zine vocabulary — a lot to own. Gives the content team a long leash: mascot evolves, pull quotes, merch, chapter drops.

Risk: most investment to maintain (mascot assets, voice discipline). Can skew precious if prose isn't sharp.

Pick C if…

You want "serious + beautiful" over "playful + memorable". Closest to Raycast/Linear/Vercel-Geist category code. Motion and process-state do all the personality work — no mascots, no stickers.

Risk: least differentiated from category; relies on execution quality (glass, motion timing) to stand out. Lime is the one thing keeping it off the generic rail.
