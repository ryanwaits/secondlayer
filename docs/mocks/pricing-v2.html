No account? Pay per call.

Built for agents. Any /v1 read can be paid with x402 — the HTTP 402 payment standard, settled on Stacks. No card, no signup, no gas: transfers are sponsored, you only hold the token.

The fine print, up front.

What stays free forever?

Anonymous rate-limited reads on every public /v1 surface, the full parquet dataset archive, and the Free tier above. Public data stays public.

What does a paid plan actually buy?

Capacity and guarantees: higher limits, more subgraphs, longer webhook retention, support and SLAs. Never access to data.

When does billing start?

When open beta ends, with notice. Usage dashboards ship first so you can see exactly where you’d land before a card is ever involved.

x402 or a subscription?

x402 suits agents and spiky, accountless workloads (pay exactly per call). Plans suit sustained workloads. They mix freely — a keyed app can still let its agents pay per call.
